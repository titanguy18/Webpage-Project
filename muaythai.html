About Muay Thai

Also known or reffered to as Thai boxing and fought in a ring just like boxing but with added moves. Where as boxing only involves punches, Muay Thai involves kicking, clinching, elbows, knees and sweeps.

Rules

The fighter would wear similar gloves to bxing but abit more flexible in the wrist area to let them be able to clich their opponents. Similar basic rules to boxing but are allowed to clinch, elbow, kick, push kick, knee but if a fighter is on the ground they get back to their corner until the referee resumes the fight.

Starting Out

Because there are a handful of moves to learn, we will teach you step by step. If you have prior experience or not or a complete beginner we got you covered. You will learn everything at your own pace to ensure the basic are covered and done right before moving on to the next step.

Training

After the basics are mastered, our trainers will let you practice for a set amount of time on your own with the punching bags. There are many types of bags, light and heavy bags, depends on how intense and tough you want the session to be.

Be Active and Get Healthier

Whilst training, you will not only get healthier and fitter but more agile and flexible. This comes from the variety of moves learned and being practiced. Your body will be more toned all around as you go along.

History

A national sport of Thailand and used historically in the ancient times by Thai and Siamese in times of war.
Developed into a sport around the 17th Century.
